The process of making our video game

December 5th, 2023 by MinotaurTheGreat and MaskMajor

First of all, welcome to our website, glad you could make it here! Second, we would be happy to share with you a short read about our thought process in making the Kat Klicker video game. You need some passion and a vision, lots and lots of patience, because this experience is fun but also humbling... Without further ado, happy reading!

The Game Development process

Ever wondered how a video-game is made? Well, we're here to explain. First of all it starts with an idea, and then the idea becomes a vision, and with the right people that vision becomes reality.

But before becoming reality it has to get through several phases of development.

In the industry, it's widely known that developing games takes time, so the developers must be as organised as possible. Therefore teams are made for each of the game's needs. Our game, Kat Klicker only has 2 developers, I and MaskMajor. But that doesn't mean we can't be organised, you see... time is a resource to be allocated, and so the development of a game happens in three main phases.

The development Phases

By three they come, by three the way opens, by the passion of the willing...Pre-production, where the teams are assembled and the project laid out. Production, where the game is made with great effort and diligence. Post-production, where the game is shared to the world.

  • Pre-production - the layout of the game.
  • Production - the construction of the game.
  • Post-production - the distribution of the game.

It's more like a journey:

  1. The hero begins the quest, receives his mission.
  2. The hero becomes well-versed and continues to grow.
  3. Finally, the hero reaches the end of the journey, his mission fullfilled.

Note that the quality assurance is part of the production stage and the launch is part of post-production stage,sometimes these two are independent if the game is really big, but they are quite important phases of game develpment nevertheless,usually, branches of the three main stages.

Some of you might ask:

What if I don't have any ideas?
Well, imitate other games you like, as we did! Add your spin to it and you have created a new game! No need to reinvent the wheel...
I don't have time/ don't know where to begin.
Little by little, you build a castle. A game usually is build piece by piece, one small thing at a time. Don't worry if it takes too long! Progress is still progress.
What if my game fails, or people won't like it?
As a storywriter myself, I have to tell you, the greatest sucess is achieving your goals, whether people like it or not. There won't always be kind people to give you criticism, but do not feel discouraged! The fact that you are doing something is a big win!

The three development stages

So we spoke a bit of the three development stages before. Let's cover them in more detail bellow...

Pre-production

This is the initial planning phase of game development before actual production begins... This includes game concept development, storyboarding, character design, level design, setting project goals, creating a game design document (GDD), prototyping, and planning the overall development process.

This stage is neccessary to establish a clear vision of the game, its mechanics, and goals before entering the full production phase.

Production

The main phase where the actual game assets are created, and the game is developed based on the plans laid out in pre-production. During this stage we do the asset creation (artwork, 3D models, sound, etc.), programming, level construction, playtesting, iteration, and refinement of game elements.

Here, we work hard to bring the game to life by implementing the design, creating content, and ensure that the gameplay is engaging and functional.

Post-production

And now we have reached the final phase of game development, that occurs after the game has been developed, where the focus shifts to testing, polishing, and preparing for release. Most of the work here is bug fixing, optimization, final testing, balancing gameplay, creating marketing materials, preparing for distribution, and addressing any remaining issues.
